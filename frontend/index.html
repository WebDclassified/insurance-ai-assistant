Insurance Domain Knowledge

🎯 Why AI in Insurance?

The insurance industry loses $80+ billion annually to fraud in the US alone. Traditional rule-based detection catches only 10-20% of fraudulent claims. AI/ML models can boost detection to 70-90% while reducing false positives, operating expenses, and claims processing cycle time by 50-70%.

📖 Insurance Glossary
Premium

The amount paid by the policyholder to the insurer for coverage. Higher risk = higher premium.

Claim

A formal request by the policyholder to the insurer for compensation for a covered loss event.

Underwriting

The process of evaluating risk to decide whether to insure someone and at what premium.

Loss Ratio

Claims paid / Premiums earned. A key measure of insurer profitability. Below 100% is profitable.

Subrogation

The insurer's right to pursue a third party that caused the loss to recover paid claim costs.

Deductible

The amount the policyholder pays out-of-pocket before insurance coverage kicks in.

Actuarial Science

Using statistics and mathematics to assess risk. The foundation of insurance pricing and reserving.

Moral Hazard

The tendency for insured individuals to take greater risks because they are protected against losses.

SIU (Special Investigation Unit)

A dedicated team within insurers that investigates suspicious claims for potential fraud.

First-Party Fraud

When the policyholder itself submits a false or inflated claim. Most common type of insurance fraud.

Soft Fraud vs Hard Fraud

Soft fraud: exaggerating a legitimate claim. Hard fraud: staging an incident or fabricating a claim entirely.

Combined Ratio

Loss ratio + expense ratio. Below 100% means underwriting profit. Industry average is ~95-100%.

🚀 Regulatory & Compliance Context

Why explainability matters: Insurance regulators (e.g., NAIC in the US, EIOPA in EU) increasingly require that AI-driven decisions be explainable. Insurers must be able to justify why a claim was flagged or denied. This is why we chose Random Forest (interpretable feature importances) over black-box models like deep neural networks.

Key regulations:

  • GDPR (EU): Right to explanation for automated decisions
  • HIPAA (US): Protection of health-related insurance data
  • NAIC Model Laws: Fair credit reporting and anti-discrimination
  • AI Act (EU, 2025): Insurance AI classified as high-risk, requires conformity assessment
